Team Weekly Ranking Help

Summary

The Team Weekly Ranking page is where a member can view a seleted Team rankings for the Season selected.

You can select a Team and Season from drop down lists.

The list will contain every game (including post games when defined) in the season selected for the team selected. The list will display the teams record for the week including scores. This will be followed by Power Rank, Offence Ranks and Defense Rank. Theh last two further broken into Over All, Score, Pass and Rush

The ranking list displays all seleted team games in week order. This can be reversed by selecting the Week button.

The Teams list scrolls inside and beneath the headers. We did this so that the headers would stay in place while the list data scrolled

Details
Selecting and viewing the results

This is where you can select Team and Season.

Selecting the Team icon will take you to the Teams official website.

The Team defaults to your favorate team if you have designeated one in your members profile (which you can updateby clicking your avatar on the top right corner of your Daredevil Ducks web page). Changing the Team will bring up rankings associated with that seaason.

The Season defaults to the current season. Changing the season will bring up rankings associated with that team.

Understanding the Ranking List

The Teams list scrolls inside and beneath the headers. We did this so that the headers would stay in place while the list data scrolled

Displayed are the selected teams weekly rankings for the selected Season and Team. This example shows four lines from the list. Each Week consists of two lines

The first column is Week. It displays the week number of the complete season in descending orders. You can toggle the order by clicking on the .

The second column is Top - Home Bottom - Away. It displays the teams competing for the selected season and week. The team in maroon is your selected team. A team score with a green background signifies a win for that team.

The third column is Score. It displays the score for the selected season and week. Clicking on the members icon will display a pop up with that members information.

The fourth column is W L T. It displays the team W = Wins, L = Losses and T = ties for the selected Season and week.

The fifth column is Power Rank which represents the combined rankings inclusive of offense and defense for the two teams.Lower number is better!

The sixth column is Offense designated by it's color. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you score against your competition. Passing is how well you pass (completions, yards, etc) against your competition. Rushing is how well you rush against your competition. Lower number is better!

The seventh column is Defense designated by it's color. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you stopped scoring against your competition. Passing is how well you stopped the pass against your competition. Rushing is how well you stopped the rush against your competition. Lower number is better!

Team Rankings.com’s Predictive Power Ratings Model uses the Predictive power ratings described in the first three parts of this series. To summarize, a computer algorithm iteratively analyzes data on every NFL game result this season. In the end, every team receives a simple numerical rating (e.g. 92.4 or 104.7) indicative of its actual, documented, proven track record at outscoring opponents.

Offensive Rankings are based on the highest to lowest Average of Yards Gained by each Team.

Defensive Rankings are based on the lowest to highest Average of Yards Gained by the Team’s Opponents.
