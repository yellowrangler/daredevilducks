Total Members Team picks for Week Help

Summary

The Total Members Team picks for Week page is where a member can view the total member picks for each team for a particular week in a particular season. This is a great way to see how other members are picking!

You can select the Season and Week from their respective drop down lists. These will automatically default to the current season and week. As soon as any selection is made the list of picked teams appears.

The list displays team pairs (home and away) for the week. The total member picks for each team are reflected under the Picks column. Team picks in green are the winners. If you place your mouse cursor over any team icon, you will see the icon turn into a picture of a graph . Selecting the team icon will bring up a window with competitive information regarding the two teams playing each other!

Clicking on the Picks number for any team brings up a list of all the members who made that team pick!

Understanding the Teams List with Picks

Displayed are the teams playing for the season and week with total member picks. This example shows two lines from the list.

The first column is Game Day. It displays the game and the day and time that the game is being played.

The second column is Home Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the home team for this game. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The third column is Picks. It displays the total member picks for the home team. A team Pick with a green background signifies a win for the home team. Clicking on the team Picks displays a pop up of all members who made those home team picks. That pop up will be be touched on later below.

The fourth column is the Away Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the away team for this game. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The fifth column is Picks. It displays the total member picks for the away team. A team Pick with a green background signifies a win for the away team. Clicking on the team Picks displays a pop up of all members who made those away team picks. That pop up will be be touched on later below.

Member Picks List Pop-up

This pop-up displays a list of all the members that made a pick for the team associated with the Picks column.

Competitive Information Pop-up

This pop-up displays competitive statistics about the two teams involved in the game. This gives you a clear depiction of how the two teams compare via Standings, Competitive chart of team offense and defense, Power rankings from the pros and a numeric representation of the offensive and defensive rankings. Let's walk down the sections

First section details standings. The two team icons appear over the team W L T Standings column. The standings display the W L T information for:

  • Overall record
  • Home record
  • Away record
  • Conference record
  • Division record

Overall record is the teams W L T record across the entire league. Home record is the teams W L T record against other teams at home. Away record is the teams W L T record against other teams when away. Conference record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their conference. Division record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their division.

The second section charts rankings. Each team is compared offense to defense (O/D or D/O). The two teams are identified by a key with coloring. This uses the data from Team Rankings with higher scores being better.

The Third, Fourth and Fifth sections display three sets of competitive rankings broken into:

  • Power Ranking
  • Offensive Ranking record
  • Defensive Ranking record

The ratings for these three sections vary from the previous chart rankings in that with these numbers, a lower number is better.

Power Rankings are the combined rankings inclusive of offense and defense for each team.

The following rankings for Offensive and Defense are further split into:

  • Overall
  • Scoring
  • Passing
  • Rushing

The Offense section. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you score against your competition. Passing is how well you pass (completions, yards, etc) against your competition. Rushing is how well you rush against your competition.

The Defense section. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you stopped scoring against your competition. Passing is how well you stopped the pass against your competition. Rushing is how well you stopped the rush against your competition.
