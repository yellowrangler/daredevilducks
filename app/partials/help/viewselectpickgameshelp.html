Member's picks for week Help

Summary

The Member's picks for week page is where a member can view the picks of any member for a particular week in a particular season.

You select the member you wish to view from the Member drop down list. The selected members icon will appear to the left. If you click on the members icon a window will pop up with that members information.

You can further refine your selection by picking Season or Week from their respective drop down lists. These will automatically default to the current season and week. As soon as any selection is made the list of picked teams appears. This list will reflect the criteria selected above.

The list displays team pairs (home and away) for the week. Selected team are maroon. Team scores in green are the winners. If you place your mouse cursor over any team icon, you will see the icon turn into a picture of a graph . Selecting the team icon will bring up a window with competitive information regarding the two teams playing each other!

Details
Selecting Members and viewing the results

This is where you select the Member whose picks you wish to see. You can further refine with options to select season and week.

First the selected member's avatar is displayed (It will default to you). Selecting the Members icon will display a pop up of the Members details.

Next is your member screen name. This is followed by 2 drop down lists. The first, is for selecting the season. This will automatically be set to the current season. But you can change it to see how the selected member did in previous seasons. The second, is for selecting the game week. This will automatically be set to the current game week. But you can change it to look at previous weeks OR go to future weeks to make selections for a different week.

Understanding the Teams List

Displayed are the teams playing for the season and week for the selected member. This example shows two lines from the list.

The first column is Game Day. It displays the game and the day and time that the game is being played.

The second column is Home Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the home team for this game. The selected team will appear maroon to show that it has been selected. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The third column is Score. It displays the home team score after the game has been played. A team score with a green background signifies a win for that team.

The fourth column W L T is the wins, losses and ties of the home team.

The fifth column is Away Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the away team for this game. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The sixth column is Score. It displays the away team score after the game has been played. A team score with a green background signifies a win for that team.

The seventh column W L T is the wins, losses and ties of the away team.

Competitive Information Pop-up

This pop-up displays competitive statistics about the two teams involved in the game. This gives you a clear depiction of how the two teams compare via Standings, Competitive chart of team offense and defense, Power rankings from the pros and a numeric representation of the offensive and defensive rankings. Let's walk down the sections

First section details standings. The two team icons appear over the team W L T Standings column. The standings display the W L T information for:

  • Overall record
  • Home record
  • Away record
  • Conference record
  • Division record

Overall record is the teams W L T record across the entire league. Home record is the teams W L T record against other teams at home. Away record is the teams W L T record against other teams when away. Conference record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their conference. Division record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their division.

The second section charts rankings. Each team is compared offense to defense (O/D or D/O). The two teams are identified by a key with coloring. This uses the data from Team Rankings with higher scores being better.

The Third, Fourth and Fifth sections display three sets of competitive rankings broken into:

  • Power Ranking
  • Offensive Ranking record
  • Defensive Ranking record

The ratings for these three sections vary from the previous chart rankings in that with these numbers, a lower number is better.

Power Rankings are the combined rankings inclusive of offense and defense for each team.

The following rankings for Offensive and Defense are further split into:

  • Overall
  • Scoring
  • Passing
  • Rushing

The Offense section. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you score against your competition. Passing is how well you pass (completions, yards, etc) against your competition. Rushing is how well you rush against your competition.

The Defense section. Overall is a combined number across all ranked areas. Scoring is how well you stopped scoring against your competition. Passing is how well you stopped the pass against your competition. Rushing is how well you stopped the rush against your competition.
