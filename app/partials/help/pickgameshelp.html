Pick Your Teams Help

Summary

The Pick Your Teams page is where a member picks his or her teams for a particular week in a particular season.

You are presented with a list displaying team pairs (home and away) for the week. You select a team by clicking on the Team Name.

You can select as many or as few teams as you wish. Just be sure to have selected all teams for the week prior to the start of the game. Any selections made after a game starts will be blocked.

After you have made your picks, you MUST save them by selecting the Save Picks button. At that point a dialog box will appear that tells you how many teams you have picked out of how many were required to be picked.

There are tools that you can use to help you make a pick. If you place your mouse cursor over any team icon, you will see the icon turn into a picture of a graph . This is to remind you that selecting the team icon will bring up a window with competative information regarding the two teams playing each other!

Details
Selecting and saving your team picks

This is where you select the season and week for your team picks. Its also where you save your picks.

First your avatar is displayed . Next is your screen name. Both of these items are not changeable by you at this time.

This is followed by 2 drop down lists. The first, is for selecting the season. This will automatically be set to the current season. But you can change it to see how you did in previous seasons. The second, is for selecting the game week. This will automatically be set to the current game week. But you can change it to look at previous weeks OR go to future weeks to make selections for a different week. In fact, you could make picks for every regular season game.

The last item is the Save Picks button. Selecting this button will save all your picks for the season and week. REMEMBER - You Must save your picks

Picking your teams

This is where you pick your teams for the selected season and week. This is also where you can view competitive information regarding the two teams playing each other

This example shows two lines from the Pick Your Teams list.

The first column is Game Day. It displays the network that is showing the game and the day and time that the game is being played. Trying to make a pick after this day and time be blocked.

The second column is Home Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the home team for this game. To pick the home team you must select the Team Name. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The third column is Score. It displays the home team score after the game has been played. A team score with a green background signifies a win for that team.

The fourth column W L T is the wins, losses and ties of the home team.

The fifth column is Away Team. It displays the team icon and team name of the away team for this game. To pick the away team you must select the Team Name. If you select the Team Icon a window will pop up with competitive information regarding the two teams. These strategic stats will be touched on later below.

The sixth column is Score. It displays the away team score after the game has been played. A team score with a green background signifies a win for that team.

The seventh column W L T is the wins, losses and ties of the away team.

Competitive Information Pop-up

This pop-up displays competitive statistics about the two teams involved in the game. This gives you a clear depiction of how the two teams compare via Standings, Competitive chart of team offense and defense, Power rankings from the pros and a numeric representation of the offensive and defensive rankings. Let's walk down the sections

First section details standings. The two team icons appear over the team W L T Standings column. The standings display the W L T information for:

  • Overall record
  • Home record
  • Away record
  • Conference record
  • Division record

Overall record is the teams W L T record across the entire league. Home record is the teams W L T record against other teams at home. Away record is the teams W L T record against other teams when away. Conference record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their conference. Division record is the teams W L T record against other teams inside their division.

The second section charts standings. Each team is compared offense to defense (O/D or D/O). The two teams are identified by a key with coloring. This uses the data from Team Rankings with higher scores being better.

The Third, Fourth and Fifth sections display three sets of power rankings broken into:

  • Power Ranking
  • Offensive Ranking record
  • Defensive Ranking record

The ratings in these sections vary from the chart rankings in that with these numbers, a lower number is better. Power rankings are the combined ranking inclusive of offense and defense for each team.The following for Offensive and Defense rankings are further split into:

  • Overall
  • Scoring
  • Passing
  • Rushing

Overall is a combined number across all defense or offense. Scoring is how well you score for offense AND how well you stopped scoring for defense. Passing is how well you pass for offense AND how well you stopped the pass for defense. Rushing is how well you rush for offense AND how well you stopped rushing for defense.
